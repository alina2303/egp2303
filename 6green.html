6. Udvidet tid

  • Blå
  • Rød
  • Grøn

Udvidet tid har altid noget med en handling at gøre. Derfor er det kun de verber, som betegner en handling, der kan sættes i udvidet tid. Men handlingsverberne får forskellig betydning alt efter, om de står i simpel eller udvidet tid, da de to bruges i forskellige situationer:

Simpel tid:
Udvidet tid:

Det, der gælder i almindelighed, (fakta).

Cars make a lot of noise.
It snows a lot in Norway.
What does he do? He's a teacher.

Det, der foregår over en begrænset periode.

The car was making a lot of noise.
Look, it's snowing!
What's he doing? He's reading.

Det, der sker regelmæssigt, gentaget, eller er en vane (tit sammen med often, never, always og sometimes).

We always have dinner at 6 o'clock.
He sleeps late on Sundays.

Noget, man er ved/er i færd med.

What were you doing at 6 o'clock?
I was having dinner.
I was sleeping when he came.

En handling, der er afsluttet i fortiden.

He read the book I gave him.
He drank all the whisky.

En handling, der foregik over en periode.

He was reading the book I gave him.
He was drinking my whisky.

Opremsninger.

First, he did the dishes, then he put on his coat and left.

Handlinger, der foregår samtidig.

John was doing the dishes, while Sue was reading a book.

Da udvidet tid kun bruges ved handlingsverber, kan man normalt ikke lave udvidet tid ved verber, der betegner:

- følelser (hate, like,...)
- tanker/holdninger (mean, believe, ...)
- sanser (smell, taste, ...)
- tilstand (belong to, own, contain, consist of, ...)

Ved verberne lie (down), sit (down) og stand (up) har simpel tid og udvidet tid hver deres helt specielle betydning:

Simpel tid betegner en handling (lægge sig, sætte sig, rejse sig).

Peter lay down (lagde sig) when the doctor arrived.
All of a sudden he stood up (rejste sig) and said he was fine.

Udvidet tid betegner en tilstand (ligge, sidde, stå).

Tony was lying down (lå ned) when the doctor arrived.
He had been standing up (havde stået op) all day.
