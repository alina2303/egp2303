3. Genitiv

  • Blå
  • Rød
  • Grøn

Engelsk har s-genitiv:

ved tidsbetegnelser,

It's been a hard day's nigh.
Let's have a few minutes'rest.

ved skibe og både,

the ship's mast
the boats' oars

ved betegnelser for sted og erhverv.

I bought it at the baker's. (underforstået the baker's shop)
We stayed at my uncle's. (underforstået my uncle's house)
I am on my way to the hairdresser's. (underforstået the hairdresser's shop)
He has been to the dentist's. (underforstået the dentist's clinic)

Hvor dansk har sammensatte substantiver har engelsk ofte s- genitiv.

a women's magazine (et dameblad)
a dog's nose (en hundesnude)
mother's milk (modermælk)
a spider's web (et edderkoppespind)

Engelsk har of-genitiv:

ved mål, mængde og antal,

a bottle of Coke
a cup of tea
two pounds of sugar
a number of students

ved geografiske betegnelser,

the country of Denmark
the Isle of Man
the town of Bethlehem
the village of Stratford

NB! Dette gælder dog ikke ved ordene lake og river.

the river Thames
Lake Michigan

ved årstider, måned og dato.

the winter of 1889
the month of April
the 24th of December

NB! Det er dog ikke nødvendigt at anvende of-genitiv ved dato-angivelse i f.eks. breve.

December 24th (læses: “December twenty-fourth”)
December 24 (læses: “December twenty-fourth”)
24th December (læses: “The twenty-fourth of December”)
24 December (læses: “The twenty-fourth of December”)

Possessive pronominer:

Også possessive pronominer udtrykker genitiv. Possessive pronominer har dog ikke apostrof, selv om de ender på -s.

He gave me his new address.
The dog has hurt its nose badly.
That is the wrong umbrella! This one is ours.
