Semikolon:

Semikolon bruges for at angive større pause end ved komma, men større sammenhæng end ved punktum.

I'm always happy to see you; in fact, I look forward to every visit.
Peter was not only sad; he was also angry.
He told me to go away; but I insisted on staying all week.

Kolon:

Kolon bruges, når man kan sige ’nemlig’.

He had three children: Peter, Joan, and Carrie.
I know what you're going to say: you didn’t know what time you were supposed to be back, right?

Tegnsætning ved direkte tale:

På engelsk står anførelsestegn (gåseøjne) altid foroven.

"Where is my mother?" Peter shouted. "I want her now!"
"Come to Alaska!" my uncle always said.

Der er aldrig kolon foran direkte tale (men som regel komma).

Then Auntie Olga said, "I wonder when Laurence will be promoted."
He said, "I never liked that husband of yours. I'm happy he's left you."

Hvis der er andre tegn i slutningen af direkte tale, står de inden for anførelsestegnene.

"Please come right away," Peter said. "My dog is very ill, and you must help him."
"Have you ever tried cooking your own dinner?" Sheila asked. "No!" he replied.
